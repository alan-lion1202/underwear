# Underwear Intelligence Project

## Mission
Through continuous research of global LGBTQ+ underwear brands, products, and market trends, build a trusted intelligence platform that helps identify the most valuable products to develop and supports the long-term growth of our B2B brand.

## Product Vision
The database exists to support product decisions, not data collection for its own sake.
Every feature, field, and page should help us understand the market, evaluate products, and build a stronger B2B brand.

## Four Design Principles
Before adding any feature, ask:
1. Does it help us understand the market better?
2. Does it help us decide which products are worth developing?
3. Does it help us serve future B2B customers better?
4. Does it help us build long-term industry knowledge?
If a feature satisfies fewer than two principles, do not add it.

## Data Rules
- Never fabricate data.
- If a field cannot be verified, use "Unknown".
- Every verified field should include a source URL.
- Do not invent brands, products, prices, emails, addresses, or manufacturing locations.
- Prefer official brand websites and official social accounts.

## Product Architecture
Research all LGBTQ+ underwear categories.
Do not exclude niche categories.
Prioritize development by stage, not by exclusion.

Phase 1 Priority:
- Men's Briefs
- Men's Trunks
- Men's Boxer Briefs
- Men's Jockstraps
- Women's Boyshorts
- Gender-neutral Boxer Briefs

Phase 2:
- Tucking underwear
- Packing underwear
- Binders
- Gaffs
- Other gender-affirming products

## UI Rules
- Keep tables compact.
- Minimize horizontal scrolling.
- Use expandable detail rows.
- Keep Brand/Product columns sticky.
- Include back button and breadcrumbs.
- Prefer visual summaries over dense tables.

## Development Rules
- ChatGPT provides research, data architecture, product strategy, and code review.
- Codex focuses on implementation.
- Do not redesign the entire project unless explicitly requested.
- Do not add authentication, Supabase, AI automation, or scraping automation unless explicitly requested.
